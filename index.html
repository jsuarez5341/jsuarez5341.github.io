About me

I am a 5th year PhD candidate in Phillip Isola's lab and the creator of Neural MMO, an open-source and computationally accessible research platform that simulates populations of agents in procedurally generated virtual worlds. The focus of my work is enabling modern agent-based learning methods to scale to increasingly complex, cognitively realistic environments.

I have run three public competitons on Neural MMO in partnership with AICrowd. The most recent two were additionally partnered with Parametrix.ai and hosted at IJCAI 2022 and NeurIPS 2022, each receiving around 500 signups and 1600 submissions. I am currently organizing a fourth competition on generalization to novel tasks in partnership with Stability.ai and CarperAI.

Outside of competitions and pure development, I run the Neural MMO community Discord, which currently has around 700 members. This includes both competition participants and academics using the platform for other research. I also run the LIFE monthly group meeting (Learning in Foundation Environments) with invited talks from researchers and developers working on new environments, platforms, and associated tooling. Message me anywhere if you want an invitation.

Previously, I graduated from Stanford for CS+AI in 2019. I took a semester off from my degree in 2018 to intern at OpenAI for 6 months, where I formalized and released the first public version of Neural MMO. Before that, I worked with Justin Johnson in Fei-Fei Li's group on dynamic visual question answering architectures. Before that, I started on language modeling research in a program run by Andrew Ng's lab.
